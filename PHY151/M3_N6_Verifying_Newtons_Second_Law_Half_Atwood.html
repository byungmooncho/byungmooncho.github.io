Verifying Newton's Second Law with a Half Atwood Machine

Synopsis Newton's second law fits in five characters — F = ma — but it makes a testable promise: double the net force on an object and its acceleration doubles; double the mass and the acceleration halves. The Half Atwood machine — a cart on a track, pulled by a hanging mass over a pulley — lets us put that promise on trial. The hanging weight supplies a known force, the whole system shares one acceleration, and your job is to predict it before the measurement pronounces the verdict.
Performance expectations Draw free-body diagrams for both objects in the Half Atwood setup · Write Newton's second law equations for each · Describe how the setup verifies the law.

The key relationships

Two objects, two equations, one shared acceleration and one shared tension. Everything follows from writing each object's story separately, then letting the string connect them.

  • T=m1a the cart, along the track — tension is its only horizontal force (frictionless track)
  • m2g−T =m2a the hanging mass, taking down as positive — gravity wins by exactly m₂a
  • a= m2g m1+m2 add the two equations — the driving weight divided by the total mass being moved
