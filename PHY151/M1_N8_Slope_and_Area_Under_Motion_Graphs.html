Slope and Area Under the Curve of Motion Graphs

Synopsis You already know that the slope of distance vs. time is speed, and the slope of velocity vs. time is acceleration. Now the reverse: the area under acceleration vs. time is the change in velocity, and the area under velocity vs. time is the change in distance. Slope takes you down the ladder; area brings you back up. One freefall measurement, three graphs — and you will verify every step of it, in both directions, with your own numbers.
Performance expectations Calculate velocity change from the area under the acceleration vs. time graph · Calculate distance change from the area under the velocity vs. time graph · Verify each result against the next graph in the chain, including a unit check at every step.

Your tasks in this note

  1. Start at the bottom: the acceleration graph of a freefalling object is a flat line at 9.8 m/s². Take the area under it up to 0.6 s — a simple rectangle — and show its units come out as velocity.
  2. Climb one rung: the velocity graph of the same fall. Its best-fit slope is 9.4 m/s² — and you should be wondering why that isn't 9.8. (You're right to wonder; that gap is measurement uncertainty, and it gets its own story later.) Read Δv off the line and check it against your rectangle.
  3. Area again: take the area under the velocity line — a triangle this time — and show its units come out as distance.
  4. Top of the ladder: the distance graph of the same fall. Read Δd directly and check it against your triangle. Three graphs, one motion, every step verified in both directions.

The key relationships

The unit check is the point: multiply the axes together and the area's units tell you what quantity you've found.

  • slope of d–t =ΔdΔt =v m divided by s gives m/s — a velocity
  • slope of v–t =ΔvΔt =a m/s divided by s gives m/s² — an acceleration
  • area under a–t =a⋅Δt =Δv m/s² times s gives m/s — a velocity change
  • area under v–t =Δd m/s times s gives m — a distance change (a triangle here, because the speed grows steadily)
