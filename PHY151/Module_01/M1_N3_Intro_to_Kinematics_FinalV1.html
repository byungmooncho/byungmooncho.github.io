Introduction to Kinematics

Synopsis Kinematics is the study of motion using physics concepts and equations.
Performance Expectations By the end of this note, you will be able to…
  • PE1 Define motion as a change in position over time, and distinguish scalar quantities (distance, speed) from vector quantities (displacement, velocity, acceleration).
  • PE2 Describe motion using position, displacement, velocity, and acceleration, and report each with correct SI units.
  • PE3 Interpret a motion (strobe) diagram to decide whether an object is at rest, moving at constant velocity, or accelerating.
  • PE4 Select the appropriate kinematic equation for a problem by matching the quantities you know against the quantity you need.
  • PE5 Recognize the three kinematic equations and identify what each one is for. You'll practice applying them to solve problems starting in Note 6.
  • PE6 Connect everyday experiences of motion — braking, falling, coasting — to the physics concepts of velocity and acceleration.
this whole note is about how position changes in time
💡You already know this

You have felt kinematics your whole life — every time speed changes, your body notices. The equations below just put numbers on things you already have intuition for:

🚗 Braking at a light Velocity drops to zero — negative acceleration you feel push you forward.
📱 A dropped phone It speeds up as it falls — constant acceleration from gravity.
🛝 A ball down a ramp Steeper ramp, faster gain in speed — bigger acceleration.
